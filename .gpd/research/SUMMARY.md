## Executive Summary

The dualised Standard Model program, culminating in Cacciapaglia and Sannino (arXiv:2407.17281, 2024), proposes that the SM can be recast as the magnetic (IR) dual of a UV "electric" theory containing only fermions and no elementary scalars, with a Pati-Salam-like gauge structure. The Higgs doublets emerge as composite mesons, the gauge bosons of the SM are the magnetic gauge fields, and the fermion mass hierarchy arises from a "scalar democracy" mechanism: a universal Yukawa coupling y combined with hierarchical Higgs VEVs generated by Planck-scale four-fermion operators. The intellectual lineage runs from 't Hooft anomaly matching (1980) through Seiberg's N=1 SQCD duality (1994-95) to Sannino's non-SUSY extensions (2009-2024), with Maekawa's Pati-Salam construction (1996) as the direct SUSY precursor. No existing lecture notes cover this material -- the project fills a genuine pedagogical gap.

The recommended approach for 8 lectures is a two-track structure: (1) a non-SUSY track covering anomaly matching, conformal windows, tumbling/MAC, and complementarity, and (2) a minimal SUSY track introducing only the machinery needed to state and motivate Seiberg duality (holomorphy, NSVZ beta function, moduli spaces). These converge in the final lectures where the dualised SM is constructed. The critical pedagogical insight is that 5 of the 9 required analytical methods are non-SUSY and can build on Michaelmas-term QFT prerequisites, minimising the SUSY overhead. The key risk is the conjectural status of non-SUSY duality: while anomaly matching is exact and constraining, it is necessary but not sufficient, and the full duality in the absence of SUSY remains unproven. Exercises must be framed as "parametric consistency checks" (the duality framework is consistent with m_t ~ O(v), Lambda_QCD ~ O(200 MeV)) rather than "predictions from first principles."

The principal pitfall is epistemic: conflating the well-established SUSY Seiberg duality (supported by holomorphy, exact beta functions, moduli space matching, and anomaly matching) with its non-SUSY extension (supported only by anomaly matching, perturbative fixed points, and consistency arguments). Every statement about the non-SUSY dualised SM must carry an appropriate qualifier. A secondary risk is the factor-of-2 convention trap in Dynkin index normalisation, which propagates to all anomaly coefficients and conformal window boundaries if not caught early.

### Critical Pitfalls

The 5 critical pitfalls, ranked by severity:

1. **Conflating conjectural non-SUSY duality with established SUSY results (P1)** -- every non-SUSY duality statement must carry "if the duality holds, then..." language. Prevention: separate SUSY lectures (where everything works) from non-SUSY extension (where it is conjectured). [SEVERITY: CRITICAL]

2. **Treating fermion mass predictions as quantitative when they are parametric (P2)** -- the dualised SM provides m_t ~ O(v) (natural top mass) and hierarchical lighter masses from VEV hierarchy, but numerical values require inputting measured parameters. Exercises must distinguish inputs from outputs. [SEVERITY: CRITICAL -- directly affects the project's acceptance criterion]

3. **Confusing 't Hooft anomaly matching (global, must match) with gauge anomaly cancellation (gauged, must vanish) (P3)** -- pedagogically distinct objects that look calculationally identical. A comparison table is mandatory. [SEVERITY: MODERATE-HIGH]

4. **Applying Seiberg duality outside the conformal window (P4)** -- the SM with N_c=3, N_f=6 is inside the SUSY conformal window (4.5 < 6 < 9) but outside the non-SUSY conformal window (lower boundary debated: N_f ~ 8-12 for SU(3)). The dualised SM construction uses larger UV gauge groups where the conformal window argument works. [SEVERITY: MODERATE-HIGH]

5. **Factor-of-2 errors from Dynkin index conventions (P11)** -- T(fund) = 1/2 (Seiberg) vs T(fund) = 1 (some refs for Dirac fermions). Propagates to ALL anomaly coefficients and conformal window boundaries. Prevention: fix convention at start, verify with b_0 = (11N-2N_f)/3. [SEVERITY: HIGH for recovery cost]

## Theoretical Connections

**1. SUSY Seiberg duality -> Non-SUSY duality (conjectured extension)** [CONJECTURED]
The entire program rests on extending the well-established N=1 SQCD duality to non-SUSY theories. Evidence: anomaly matching solutions (Sannino 2009), perturbative fixed points in the magnetic theory (Antipin et al. 2013), emergent SUSY at fixed points, large-N orientifold planar equivalence (Armoni-Shifman-Unsal 2008). The Sannino-Schechter toy model (1997) showed how QCD degrees of freedom emerge from SUSY auxiliary fields after SUSY breaking, but Cheng-Shadmi (1998) identified tensions at large breaking.

**2. Seiberg duality <-> Complementarity** [ESTABLISHED within SUSY; CONJECTURED non-SUSY]
The magnetic theory's Higgs phase (where scalar mesons get VEVs) is identified via complementarity with the electric theory's confining phase. This is rigorous for fundamental-representation Higgs fields (Fradkin-Shenker theorem). For the SM with partial gauge breaking (SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1) -> SU(3) x U(1)), the argument requires more care.

**3. Scalar democracy <-> Meson matrix VEV hierarchy** [ESTABLISHED as mechanism]
Hill et al. (2019) showed that with multiple Higgs doublets sharing a universal Yukawa coupling, the fermion mass hierarchy maps directly to the Higgs VEV hierarchy. In the dualised SM, the meson matrix M^i_j provides exactly this structure: 18 Higgs doublets with a universal magnetic Yukawa coupling y, and hierarchical VEVs generated by Planck-scale operators.

**4. N=2 -> N=1 mass deformation -> Origin of magnetic degrees of freedom** [ESTABLISHED]
Argyres-Plesser-Seiberg (1996) showed that N=1 Seiberg duality can be derived by deforming N=2 SQCD. The magnetic quarks are identified with monopoles/dyons on the N=2 Coulomb branch. This provides the deepest justification for why the duality exists, though the full N=2 machinery is too heavy for an 8-lecture course.

**5. Anomaly matching as cross-validation across descriptions** [ESTABLISHED]
't Hooft anomaly matching provides the only rigorous cross-check between electric and magnetic descriptions that works in both SUSY and non-SUSY. The 6 independent anomaly coefficients for SU(N_f)_L x SU(N_f)_R x U(1)_B x U(1)_R must match exactly, with no free parameters.

## Implications for Research Plan

Based on combined analysis, the suggested phase structure reflects the logical dependencies of the physics and the pedagogical constraints of an 8-lecture course.

### Phase 1: Foundations -- Anomaly Matching and Conformal Windows

**Rationale:** Anomaly matching is the prerequisite for everything. It is non-SUSY, builds directly on Michaelmas QFT, and provides the core tool for all subsequent duality checks. The conformal window analysis determines when duality applies and motivates the entire program.
**Delivers:** Lectures 1-2 in LaTeX; exercises on SM anomaly cancellation and Banks-Zaks fixed point calculation.
**Validates:** SM anomaly cancellation (exact); b_0 formula; conformal window boundaries for SU(N_c) with N_f fundamentals.
**Avoids:** Pitfall P3 (anomaly matching vs cancellation confusion) by establishing the distinction from the start. Pitfall P11 (Dynkin index conventions) by fixing T(fund) = 1/2 in a conventions section.
**Methods:** 't Hooft anomaly matching (Method 1), Banks-Zaks (Method 3).

### Phase 2: SUSY Duality Machinery

**Rationale:** Seiberg duality requires minimal N=1 SUSY background (superfields, superpotential, holomorphy). This phase introduces only the SUSY needed for the duality statement, not a full SUSY course. The N=2 to N=1 deformation provides conceptual grounding.
**Delivers:** Lectures 3-5 in LaTeX; exercises on anomaly matching verification for Seiberg duality (6 independent checks), mass deformation flow.
**Validates:** All 6 anomaly coefficients match between SU(N_c) and SU(N_f - N_c); mass deformation produces correct flow between dual theories.
**Avoids:** Pitfall P6 (SUSY background assumption) by defining every concept at first use. Pitfall P7 (naive EM duality analogy) by listing explicit differences.
**Builds on:** Phase 1 anomaly matching machinery.
**Methods:** Seiberg duality (Method 2), Holomorphy (Method 6), N=2 deformation (Method 7, executive summary only).

### Phase 3: Non-Perturbative Dynamics -- Tumbling and Complementarity

**Rationale:** Tumbling and complementarity provide the physical mechanism connecting the electric and magnetic descriptions to observable particles. These are non-SUSY methods that give physical intuition before the Sannino construction.
**Delivers:** Lecture 6 in LaTeX; exercise on MAC criterion applied to simple chiral gauge theory.
**Validates:** Condensation patterns consistent with anomaly matching at each tumbling step; complementarity identifies correct quantum numbers.
**Avoids:** Pitfall P5 (confusing tumbling with duality) by presenting each concept with clear definition and comparison table.
**Builds on:** Phase 1 (anomaly matching) and Phase 2 (duality framework).
**Methods:** MAC/Tumbling (Method 4), Complementarity (Method 5).

### Phase 4: The Dualised Standard Model

**Rationale:** This is the culmination. All machinery is in place. The dualised SM construction applies Seiberg-type duality to the SM gauge sectors via a Pati-Salam embedding. The quasi-SUSY spectrum, scalar democracy mechanism, and parametric mass matching are developed.
**Delivers:** Lectures 7-8 in LaTeX; exercises on: (a) constructing the dual gauge group from SM quantum numbers, (b) anomaly matching for the dualised SM, (c) deriving m_t ~ O(v) from universal Yukawa + leading VEV, (d) parametric scaling of Lambda_QCD from RG running below the duality scale.
**Validates:** Dual gauge group SU(N_f - N_c) = SU(3) for QCD sector; anomaly matching for complete construction; parametric consistency of m_t ~ 173 GeV, v ~ 246 GeV, Lambda_QCD ~ 200 MeV.
**Avoids:** Pitfall P1 (conjectural status) by maintaining "if the duality holds" language. Pitfall P2 (quantitative vs parametric) by framing exercises correctly. Pitfall P8 (Chan-Tsou confusion) by explicit disambiguation. Pitfall P9 (incomplete flavour structure) by presenting flavour as a framework with open questions.
**Builds on:** All previous phases.
**Methods:** Dualised SM construction (Method 8), RG scale matching (Method 9).

### Phase Ordering Rationale

- **Anomaly matching before duality:** Anomaly matching is a non-SUSY tool that constrains every subsequent construction. It must come first.
- **SUSY basics before non-SUSY applications:** The SUSY case is where duality is established; the non-SUSY case is where it is conjectured. Presenting SUSY first gives students the firm ground before the conjecture.
- **Tumbling/MAC between SUSY and dualised SM:** Provides physical intuition for how the electric theory produces the SM spectrum, bridging the formal duality with observable physics.
- **Dualised SM last:** Requires all prior machinery. The exercises in Phase 4 draw on every previous method.
