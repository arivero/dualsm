      - This provides a conceptual bridge (not a full derivation) between the SUSY meson and the physical hadronic spectrum
      - Cite 1010.4105

    **Section 5: Connection to Chiral Perturbation Theory (~4 pages)**
    - The ChPT Lagrangian: L_ChPT = (f_pi^2/4) Tr(d_mu U^dag d^mu U) + (f_pi^2/4) Tr(chi^dag U + U^dag chi)
    - Where U = exp(2i pi^a T^a / f_pi) parametrises the Goldstone bosons of SU(N_f)_L x SU(N_f)_R -> SU(N_f)_V
    - The connection: the pseudo-Goldstone bosons pi^a in the ChPT field U are exactly the angular (phase) modes of the meson condensate <M_{ij}>
    - For N_f = 2: 3 Goldstone bosons (pi^+, pi^-, pi^0) -- the pion triplet
    - For N_f = 3: 8 Goldstone bosons (pi^+, pi^-, pi^0, K^+, K^-, K^0, K-bar^0, eta) -- the pseudoscalar octet
    - For N_f = 6: formally 35 Goldstone bosons, but the t, b, c quarks are too heavy for ChPT to apply -- only the u, d, s sector is in the ChPT regime
    - **GMOR relation:** m_pi^2 f_pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d) <q-bar q>
      With f_pi = 92.1 MeV, <q-bar q> ~ -(270 MeV)^3, m_u + m_d ~ 8.5 MeV (MS-bar at 2 GeV):
      m_pi^2 = (m_u + m_d) * (270 MeV)^3 / f_pi^2 = 8.5 * (270)^3 / (92.1)^2 MeV^2
      = 8.5 * 1.97e7 / 8482 MeV^2 ~ 19700 MeV^2 -> m_pi ~ 140 MeV. Check!
    - **Heavy mesons and HQET:**
      - The ChPT description breaks down for heavy quarks (c, b)
      - D and B mesons are described by heavy quark effective theory (HQET): expand in 1/m_Q
      - In the dual picture, the heavy meson entries M_{ij} with i or j = 4,5 still correspond to physical mesons, but their dynamics is governed by HQET, not ChPT
      - Briefly state this limitation; do not attempt to derive HQET from the dual picture
