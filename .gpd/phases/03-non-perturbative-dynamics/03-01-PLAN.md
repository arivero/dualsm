    **Section structure** (following the RESEARCH.md recommended approach):

    **Section 1: Chiral gauge theories.**
    - Define chiral gauge theories (fermion content not forming a real representation of the gauge group).
    - Contrast with vector-like theories (like QCD) where chiral symmetry breaking is the standard expectation.
    - State that chiral theories can undergo dynamical symmetry breaking through condensation of fermion bilinears.
    - Preview: the rest of this lecture develops the tools (MAC, tumbling, complementarity) for analysing strong dynamics in such theories.

    **Section 2: The Most Attractive Channel (MAC) criterion.**
    - Present the one-gluon exchange argument: at leading order in perturbation theory, the attraction between two fermions in representations R_1 and R_2 forming a condensate in channel R_c is proportional to Delta C_2 = C_2(R_1) + C_2(R_2) - C_2(R_c).
    - The channel with the LARGEST Delta C_2 condenses first.
    - Use the locked Casimir values: C_2(fund) = (N^2-1)/(2N), C_2(adj) = N, C_2(sym) = (N+2)(N-1)/N, C_2(asym) = (N-2)(N+1)/N. Reference \Cfund macro from preamble.tex.
    - State limitations EXPLICITLY in a warningbox:
      (a) MAC is a conjecture, not a theorem.
      (b) The one-gluon exchange argument is perturbative reasoning applied at strong coupling.
      (c) Counterexamples exist where competing channels have comparable attraction (cite Kosower 1983).
      (d) MAC identifies the condensation CHANNEL but not the condensation SCALE.
    - Use language: "The MAC criterion SUGGESTS that..." not "The condensate forms in the MAC."

    **Section 3: Tumbling -- the SU(5) worked example.**
    - Work through the Georgi-Glashow SU(5) model with fermions in 10 + 5-bar (standard GUT content).
    - Step 1: Enumerate candidate condensation channels for 10 x 10 bilinears (tensor product decomposition). The 10 is the antisymmetric rank-2 representation of SU(5).
    - Step 2: Compute Delta C_2 for each candidate channel. Identify the MAC.
      - For SU(5): C_2(10) = C_2(antisym_2) = (5-2)(5+1)/5 = 18/5. C_2(5) = C_2(fund) = (25-1)/10 = 12/5.
      - The most attractive channel for 10 x 10 has R_c = 5-bar: Delta C_2 = 18/5 + 18/5 - 12/5 = 24/5.
    - Step 3: After condensation in the 5-bar channel, SU(5) breaks to SU(4). Identify the surviving massless fermions by anomaly matching.
    - Step 4: VERIFY anomaly matching at this step. Count the anomaly coefficients of the surviving global symmetry in the UV theory and the broken theory. They must match exactly (connect to Lecture 1 machinery, cite it rather than re-derive).
    - State: this is "tumbling" -- sequential condensation where each step breaks the gauge group further, and the residual theory is analysed again by MAC.

    **Section 4: Complementarity (Fradkin-Shenker).**
    - State the Fradkin-Shenker theorem: for gauge theories with a scalar (Higgs) field in the FUNDAMENTAL representation of the gauge group, the Higgs phase and the confinement phase are analytically connected -- there is no phase transition separating them.
    - Physical consequence: confined-phase composites can be identified one-to-one with Higgs-phase elementary fields by matching quantum numbers.
    - Conditions (state explicitly in a warningbox):
      (a) The scalar must be in the fundamental representation.
      (b) The gauge group must be completely broken (no residual gauge symmetry).
    - Limitations (state explicitly):
      (a) For adjoint Higgs or higher-representation Higgs, phase transitions CAN separate the phases.
      (b) For partial gauge breaking (as in the SM: SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y -> U(1)_EM), complementarity requires additional arguments beyond the Fradkin-Shenker theorem.
      (c) Reference Shifman-Yung (arXiv:1704.06201): the Seiberg-Witten quark confinement phase is NOT analytically connected to the Higgs phase.

    **Section 5: Connection to duality.**
    - Show how tumbling + complementarity provides a PHYSICAL PICTURE connecting the electric theory (confining, with a condensation cascade) to the magnetic theory (Higgs phase, with elementary scalars that are the composite mesons).
    - The confined composites of the electric theory are identified (via complementarity) with the elementary fields of the magnetic Higgs phase.
    - This physical picture MOTIVATES the Seiberg duality from Lecture 3, providing an intuitive mechanism for why the duality should work.
    - Prepare the ground for Lecture 6: in SUSY, this picture is made rigorous by holomorphy and exact results. In non-SUSY, we lose the rigorous tools but the physical picture survives as a CONJECTURE.
