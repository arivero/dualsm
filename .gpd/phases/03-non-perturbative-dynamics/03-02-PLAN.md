    **Section 2: Sannino-Schechter toy model (1997).**
    - Present the insight from hep-th/9708113: in the SUSY effective Lagrangian for SQCD, QCD-like degrees of freedom emerge from the auxiliary fields after SUSY breaking.

    **Section 3: The non-SUSY QCD dual (Sannino, 2009).**
    - Present arXiv:0907.1364. Key result: non-trivial solutions to 't Hooft anomaly matching conditions exist for non-SUSY SU(N_c) with N_f fundamental Dirac flavours.
    - The proposed dual gauge group is SU(N_f - N_c) -- the SAME as in SUSY Seiberg duality.
    - Present the anomaly matching: the dual matter content is chosen such that ALL independent 't Hooft anomaly coefficients match between the electric and magnetic descriptions.

    - Use conjectural language: "If the non-SUSY duality holds, then..." for all predictions.

    **Section 4: The Standard Model as a magnetic dual (Sannino, 2011).**
    - Present arXiv:1102.5100. Key result: the SM can be viewed as the magnetic dual of a purely fermionic electric theory with Pati-Salam-like gauge structure.
    - Three key consequences (all conjectural):
      (a) No elementary scalars in the UV = no hierarchy problem (the Higgs doublets are composite mesons of the electric theory)
      (b) N_gen >= 3 follows from requiring a non-abelian dual gauge group
      (c) Yukawa couplings have a common origin in the magnetic superpotential-like coupling W_mag ~ M q q-tilde
    - Reference Maekawa-Takahashi (hep-ph/9510426) as the DIRECT SUSY precursor: they constructed the Seiberg dual of a SUSY theory with Pati-Salam gauge group, obtaining natural top mass hierarchy and natural mu parameter. Sannino's construction extends this to non-SUSY.
    - Use conjectural language: "If the SM is the magnetic dual of a Pati-Salam-like electric theory, then..."

    **Section 5: Emergent SUSY at fixed points (Antipin-Mojaza-Pica-Sannino, 2013).**
    - Present arXiv:1105.1510 (note: published 2011 as preprint, full analysis 2013). Key result: in the magnetic dual theory augmented by an adjoint fermion, SUSY fixed points emerge from non-SUSY RG flow without fine-tuning.
    - This supports the "quasi-supersymmetric" spectrum of the magnetic theory: even without imposing SUSY, the IR dynamics organises fields into SUSY-like multiplets at the fixed point.
    - State the limitation explicitly: "This is a perturbative result valid in the Veneziano limit (N_c, N_f -> infinity with N_f/N_c fixed). Whether the emergent SUSY survives at finite N (and in particular at the SM values N_c = 3, N_f = 6) is an open question with uncontrolled corrections."
    - Mention that Armoni-Shifman-Unsal (arXiv:0801.0762) provide a complementary large-N argument for non-SUSY duality via orientifold planar equivalence, also limited to the large-N regime.
    - Use conjectural language: "The perturbative analysis suggests that..." not "shows" or "proves."

    **Section 6: The central vulnerability.**
    This section is the CONCLUDING POINT of the lecture and must be given prominent treatment (not buried in a paragraph).

    - State the central vulnerability explicitly: "Non-SUSY duality has NO independent cross-validation beyond 't Hooft anomaly matching."
    - In SUSY, duality is supported by FOUR independent pillars working together:
      (1) Anomaly matching (2) Holomorphy (3) Moduli space matching (4) NSVZ exact beta function
    - In non-SUSY, ONLY anomaly matching survives. This is necessary but not sufficient.
    - State: "The construction POSTULATES the duality and checks consistency via anomaly matching. It does not PROVE the duality. The Sannino programme is the simplest CONSISTENT framework that produces the correct SM structure, but consistency is not proof."
    - Address the student question "Then why do we believe it?":
      (a) Anomaly matching is highly constraining -- the number of consistent solutions is small.
      (b) The SUSY evidence from Seiberg duality is compelling and suggests the duality structure may survive SUSY breaking.
      (c) The framework produces non-trivial predictions (N_gen >= 3, composite Higgs, common Yukawa origin) that are consistent with observation.
      (d) But belief is not proof. Independent validation (lattice, new formal tools, experimental signatures) is needed.
    - Mention recent work: Bi and Terning (arXiv:2208.07842) continue to explore non-SUSY duality candidates, verifying 21 anomaly matching conditions for chiral gauge theories with Spin(8) dual. The field remains active.
    - End with a previewbox: "In Lectures 7-8, we will assume the non-SUSY duality and construct the dualised Standard Model following Cacciapaglia and Sannino. Every prediction derived there is conditional on the conjectured duality being correct."
